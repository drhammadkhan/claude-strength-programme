Start here

Use this as a low-friction strength habit — enough stimulus to matter, not so much soreness that it interferes with cycling or life.

🎯

Goal

Build strength, preserve muscle, improve posture, support mood, and protect the lower back.

⚡

Effort

Finish most sets with 2–3 good reps in reserve. Smooth form beats fatigue chasing.

🏋️

Equipment

One kettlebell, a pair of dumbbells, and enough floor space to move cleanly.

⚠

Safety: Stop if you get chest pain, faintness, severe breathlessness, sharp joint pain, neurological symptoms, or back pain that does not settle. Get medical advice if unsure.

The plan

Six-month build

Each phase has one job. Stay with the phase you're in, then progress when the work feels repeatable.

1
Weeks 1–4

Learn the movements

Three sessions weekly. Two to three rounds. Easy weights. Hold swings until the hinge is comfortable.

2
Weeks 5–8

Build consistency

Three sessions weekly. Three rounds. Add reverse lunges and increase reps gradually.

3
Weeks 9–12

Practice swings

Start with two sets of ten swings, then complete the normal strength work.

4
Weeks 13–16

Load progressively

Three to four rounds. Add load once twelve clean reps are comfortable.

5
Weeks 17–20

Condition strength

Use circuits with a swing, squat or hinge, row or press, and carry or core slot.

6
Weeks 21–24

Maintain the habit

Repeat A/B sessions, progress slowly, and keep the routine alive without chasing exhaustion.

Training

The sessions

Warm up, alternate Session A and Session B, and add swing practice from week 9 once technique is solid. Default rhythm: Monday, Wednesday, Friday.

Warm-up · 2 min

  • March on spot — 30 s
  • Arm circles — 30 s
  • Hip hinges — 30 s
  • Bodyweight squats — 30 s

Never skip. Two minutes protects joints and primes the movement patterns you're about to train.

Reference

Exercise library

Open any exercise for a plain-English cue and a demonstration video.

Goblet squat +

Hold the kettlebell close to your chest. Sit down between your hips as if lowering into a chair. Keep your chest up and stand by pushing the floor away.

Overhead press +

Hold dumbbells at shoulder height. Brace your stomach and press them overhead without leaning back. Lower under control.

One-arm row +

Support one hand on a sturdy surface. Pull the weight towards your ribs, keep the movement controlled, and avoid twisting. If nothing feels stable, use a hip-hinged two-arm dumbbell row instead.

Romanian deadlift +

Hold weights in front of you. Push your hips backwards while keeping your back neutral. Feel the hamstring stretch, then stand tall.

Reverse lunge +

Step backwards, lower under control, then push through the front foot to stand. Start bodyweight only if needed.

Floor press +

Lie on the floor holding dumbbells. Press them up like a bench press. The floor limits shoulder strain and makes this safe without a spotter.

Farmer carry +

Pick up weights, stand tall, shoulders back, core tight, and walk slowly. Trains grip, posture, trunk stability, and mental resilience.

Kettlebell swing +

This is a hip hinge, not a squat. The hips drive the bell forward — arms stay relaxed like hooks. Learn this slowly before adding to your sessions.

Guidelines

Progression rules

The rules keep the plan boring in the best way — steady, repeatable, and hard to derail.

  1. 01Start lighter than your ego wants.
  2. 02When all reps feel easy for two sessions, increase reps before increasing weight.
  3. 03When you can do 12 good reps, increase the weight slightly and return to 8 reps.
  4. 04Never progress a movement that feels technically messy.
  5. 05With cycling-heavy weeks, do two sessions rather than forcing four.
  6. 06The programme succeeds when it becomes boringly repeatable.
⚡

Minimum effective week: One Session A and one Session B. That is the fallback plan during busy weeks.
